## What problem does AiWhisperers solve

Organizations producing or commissioning AI-generated content face a
growing requirement to **prove provenance**: where the content came
from, who operated which model, and that it was not altered after
generation.

Existing approaches (visible watermarks, metadata tags, platform
attestations) have limitations: removable, forgeable, dependent on
single-vendor platforms. AiWhisperers provides an alternative —
**cryptographic content provenance certificates** that are:

- Signed with Ed25519 (256-bit elliptic curve, EdDSA)
- Backwards-compatible with P-256 verification
- Self-sovereign (no central authority, no platform dependency)
- Independently verifiable by anyone with the public key
- Linkable through a forest-of-trust certification model
- Auditable through a deterministic SHA-256 payload structure

The technical core lives in two modules:
- **[AiWPass](./apps/_pass/)** — operator identity and certificate generation
- **[AiWVerify](./apps/_verify/)** — independent verification of certificates and chains

## EU AI Act Article 50 mapping

Article 50 of EU Regulation 2024/1689 (effective 2 August 2026)
requires providers of AI systems generating synthetic content to
ensure outputs are marked as artificially generated, in a
machine-readable format, and detectable.

The Draft Code of Practice for General-Purpose AI Models (under
discussion at the time of writing) explicitly permits, for text
content, **digitally signed manifests that formally guarantee the
origin of the content** as an alternative to embedded watermarks.

AiWhisperers provides exactly this primitive:

| Article 50 requirement | AiWhisperers component |
|------------------------|------------------------|
| Marking AI-generated content | `aiw_contracts_v1` certificate format |
| Machine-readable format | JSON with canonical SHA-256 payload |
| Detectable / verifiable | Ed25519 signature, public key recoverable |
| Persistent attribution | Operator Chain (forest-of-trust) |
| Tamper-evident | SHA-256 chained provenance |

AiWhisperers is not a certified compliance product. It is an
**open-source primitive** that an organization can integrate into
its compliance architecture, audit independently, and operate
without vendor lock-in.

## Technical specifications

### Certificate format

A provenance certificate (`aiw_contracts_v1`) contains:

```json
{
  "operator":         "Display name of operator",
  "model":            "Identifier of AI system used",
  "version":          "Specific model version",
  "date":             "Human-readable date",
  "pubkey":           "<base64 Ed25519 public key>",
  "sha_declaration":  "<hex SHA-256 of declaration>",
  "sha_warunki":      "<hex SHA-256 of terms>",
  "timestamp":        "ISO 8601 UTC timestamp",
  "signature":        "<base64 Ed25519 signature>"
}
```

The signature is computed over a canonically serialized payload
with deterministic field ordering — ensuring reproducible SHA-256
hashes across implementations.

For the full schema, see [`apps/_pass/`](./apps/_pass/) and
the boot document [`_pass-boot.md`](./apps/_pass/_pass-boot.md).

### Verification

Verification requires:

1. The certificate (typically embedded with or alongside the content)
2. The operator's public key (typically distributed via the
   operator's profile export)
3. Optionally, the lineage chain documenting derivation history

Verification produces a binary result (valid / invalid) with
detailed diagnostic output. The verifier ([AiWVerify](./apps/_verify/))
runs entirely client-side — no external service required.

### Cryptographic choices

- **Ed25519** — primary signature algorithm. Modern, fast,
  standardized (RFC 8032), resistant to side-channel attacks.
- **P-256 (ECDSA)** — fallback for backwards compatibility with
  certificates generated by earlier versions. Verification
  supports both.
- **SHA-256** — for all payload hashing.
- **PBKDF2 (300,000 iterations, SHA-256) + AES-256-GCM** — for
  protecting the private key at rest via PIN/passphrase.

All cryptographic primitives use Web Crypto API in browser contexts
and standard libraries (`libsodium`, Python `cryptography`, Node
`crypto`) for server contexts. No custom cryptographic code; all
primitives are standards-based.

## Operator Chain (forest-of-trust model)

AiWhisperers does not use a centralized certificate authority.
Instead, operators form a **forest of trust** through mutual
certification:

- An operator's first certificate is **self-signed** (root)
- An operator may **certify another operator** — issuing an
  `aiw_certification_v1` document signed by their private key
- A child operator records this certification in their profile
- Verification traces the chain back to root(s)

This is the same architectural pattern as PGP web-of-trust and
SSH key authentication, adapted to AI content provenance. There
is no single point of failure; no entity can revoke another's
identity unilaterally.

For organizations: this means you can establish your own root of
trust internally, certify your operators, and produce content
whose provenance is verifiable by anyone without exposing your
internal hierarchy beyond what you choose to publish.

## Integration patterns

### Pattern 1: Operator-side signing

Your operators run a local AiWPass instance, generate keys, and
sign each piece of AI-generated content as it leaves their
workflow. Verification is open to anyone.

**Use case:** content marketing, journalism, research publication,
legal document drafting.

### Pattern 2: Organizational root + delegated operators

Your organization holds a root key (in a hardware security module
or equivalent), certifies internal operators, and publishes the
root public key. All organizational content is traceable to the
root via the certification chain.

**Use case:** enterprise compliance with Article 50, regulated
industries (financial services, healthcare, legal).

### Pattern 3: Embedded in pipeline

AiWPass primitives are integrated directly into your AI generation
pipeline. Certificates are produced server-side at the moment of
generation and attached to outputs.

**Use case:** SaaS platforms generating content at scale.

For pattern 3 specifically, the AGPL-3.0 obligation to disclose
modifications to network users may not fit the business model.
See [COMMERCIAL.md](./COMMERCIAL.md) for the commercial licensing
path.

## What this project is NOT

To avoid wasted evaluation time:

- **NOT a hosted SaaS.** AiWhisperers is software; you run it.
- **NOT a certification authority.** No central trust anchor exists.
- **NOT a watermark embedder.** AiWhisperers signs separately from
  the content; watermarking is complementary, not provided here.
- **NOT a deepfake detector.** AiWhisperers verifies what an
  operator signed; it cannot tell you whether content **without** a
  certificate is AI-generated.
- **NOT a complete compliance solution.** Article 50 compliance
  involves more than provenance marking (transparency obligations,
  fundamental rights impact assessments, etc.). AiWhisperers is
  one component of a compliance architecture, not the architecture
  itself.
