You may use, study, modify, and self-host AiWhisperers freely under

## When AGPL-3.0 may not fit your use case

AGPL-3.0 has one significant practical implication: if you **deploy
a modified version** of AiWhisperers as a network service (SaaS) to
third-party users, you must make your modified source code
available to those users under AGPL-3.0.

This is by design — it ensures that improvements to AiWhisperers
remain available to the community even when the software is used
through a network interface.

For some commercial deployments, this is incompatible with the
business model — typically:

- Embedding AiWhisperers into a proprietary product where source
  disclosure would expose unrelated proprietary code
- Offering a commercial SaaS built on AiWhisperers without releasing
  the operational modifications as open source
- Combining AiWhisperers code with code under license that is
  incompatible with AGPL-3.0

For these cases, a separate commercial license is available.

## What the commercial license offers

A commercial license, negotiated separately, grants:

**(a)** A non-AGPL alternative — you may use, modify, and deploy
the licensed code under terms compatible with proprietary software
development, without the AGPL source disclosure obligation.

**(b)** Trademark permission — specific, scoped permission to use
AiWhisperers trademarks within the agreed commercial context.

**(c)** Support tier — depending on the agreement, direct support,
priority bug fixes, custom features, or roadmap influence.

**(d)** Indemnification — depending on the agreement, IP indemnity
provisions appropriate to the deployment scale.

Commercial license terms are negotiated individually and depend on
the scope of use, organization size, and revenue scale.

## Who should consider a commercial license

If any of the following describes your intended use, please get in
touch before building:

- You are a for-profit company planning to integrate AiWhisperers
  components into a commercial product or service
- You are building a hosted SaaS that exposes AiWhisperers
  functionality to your customers and source disclosure under AGPL
  would conflict with your business strategy
- You are training AI models on AiWhisperers content for commercial
  deployment
- You are deploying AiWhisperers internally within a for-profit
  organization at scale and prefer commercial-grade licensing,
  support, and indemnification
- You want to use AiWhisperers branding or the "AiW*" module naming
  prefix in your product naming, beyond fair-use attribution

## Who likely does NOT need a commercial license

The following uses are typically fine under AGPL-3.0 with no
separate license needed:

- Individual operators using AiWhisperers tools for personal
  practice
- Academic researchers using the methodology and citing properly
- Non-profit organizations using the tools internally
- Companies using AiWhisperers internally **without distributing
  modifications externally** (internal use does not trigger AGPL
  network clause)
- Open-source projects that are themselves AGPL-3.0 compatible

If you are uncertain whether your use requires a commercial license,
ask. A short email saves both of us time.

## Pricing

There is no public price list. Commercial licensing is negotiated
per case and depends on the factors above. For small commercial
deployments the cost is intentionally accessible; for
enterprise-scale use it reflects the value derived. The pricing
philosophy is **fair and proportional**, not extractive.

The principle: AiWhisperers is open source because the methodology
should be available to anyone who wants to learn or build with it.
Commercial licensing exists to make that sustainable, not to
gate-keep.

---

## Trademark note

A commercial license for the **code** does not automatically grant
expanded trademark rights. Trademark usage in commercial contexts
is typically addressed within the same negotiation, but is governed
by `TRADEMARK.md` and is conceptually separate from the code
license.
