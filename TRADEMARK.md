## Overview

The AGPL-3.0 license governs the **source code and documentation** of
the AiWhisperers ecosystem. This document, separately, governs the use
of AiWhisperers **trademarks and brand names**.

Trademark law and copyright law are independent. An open-source
license grants you the right to use, modify, and redistribute the
code; it does not grant you the right to use the project's name or
marks to brand your own work.

This separation is standard practice across open-source projects
(e.g., Mozilla Firefox, Python, PostgreSQL, Linux kernel). The intent
is not restrictive — it is to prevent confusion in the community
about which work is the official upstream and which is a third-party
derivative.

## Permitted uses (no permission required)

### 1. Accurate factual attribution

You may state that your work uses, is derived from, or is compatible
with AiWhisperers. Examples:

- "Built using AiWhisperers Methodology"
- "Compatible with AiWhisperers Schema v1.0"
- "Forked from AiWhisperers v2.0"
- "Implements the AiWhisperers session protocol"

### 2. Academic and journalistic citation

You may name and discuss AiWhisperers in research papers, blog posts,
news articles, conference talks, books, and similar works. Standard
citation practice applies.

### 3. Nominative fair use

You may use the AiWhisperers name to refer to AiWhisperers (e.g.,
"this plugin works with AiWhisperers"). You may not use it in a way
that suggests sponsorship or endorsement.

### 4. Personal forks and modifications

You may fork the repository and make changes for personal,
educational, or research use without seeking trademark permission,
provided that:

- You do not publicly market your fork as "AiWhisperers" or as the
  official version
- If publishing the fork, you follow the renaming rules below

## Renaming rules for public modifications

If you publicly release a Modified Version of AiWhisperers (whether
as a fork, distribution, hosted service, or commercial product), you
must:

**(a) Change the user-facing project name.** The Modified Version
must have a distinct name that is clearly different from
"AiWhisperers".

- Acceptable: "Whisperflow", "RelAIte", "MyCompanyChain", "ProvenanceForge"
- Not acceptable: "AiWhisperers Pro", "AiWhisperers Enterprise",
  "AiWhisperers+", "AiWhisperers Community Edition", "Open
  AiWhisperers"

**(b) Do NOT use the "AiW*" prefix for new modules.** The "AiW*"
namespace is reserved for upstream modules. A Modified Version
adding new components must use its own naming convention.

- Acceptable: "MyForkFlow", "WhisperCustom"
- Not acceptable: "AiWBridge", "AiWConnect", "AiWForge" (these would
  imply they are part of the upstream AiWhisperers family)

**(c) Change visible branding.** Logos, color schemes specifically
associated with AiWhisperers, and the named glyph system from
`_schema` should be replaced with your own. The internal Schema v1.0
structure is under AGPL and may stay; the visual identity of
upstream should not be reproduced.

**(d) Preserve attribution as required by the AGPL additional terms
in `LICENSE`** (the "Based on AiWhisperers..." notice). This
requirement is separate from trademark — you preserve attribution
to the original, while your fork has its own distinct identity.

This is the same pattern used by Firefox/Iceweasel, Chromium/Chrome,
and RHEL/CentOS — it is not designed to discourage forks, but to
keep clear who maintains what.

This policy exists to protect users and contributors of the

adoption. If you are uncertain whether a planned use falls within

email to denis.czulinski@gmail.com resolves most ambiguities within
days.

The Owner reserves the right to enforce trademark rights through
legal means, but strongly prefers to resolve issues through direct
communication.
