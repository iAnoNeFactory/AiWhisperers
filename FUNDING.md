## Why this document

AiWhisperers is open-source software developed by an individual
maintainer. The project is licensed under AGPL-3.0 with additional
trademark protections — free to use, study, modify, and self-host
by anyone.

Sustaining this kind of work over time requires resources. This
document explains the ways supporters can contribute, the
expectations on either side, and what the funds are used for.

If you are a for-profit organization considering commercial use that
goes beyond AGPL terms, see [COMMERCIAL.md](./COMMERCIAL.md) — that
is a separate track from the donation channels described here.

## Ways to support

### 1. GitHub Sponsors (recommended for most supporters)

The simplest way to contribute is through GitHub Sponsors:

**https://github.com/sponsors/iAnoNeFactory**

GitHub Sponsors offers monthly recurring or one-time donations, with
transparent processing and no platform fees on the supporter side.
Both individuals and organizations can sponsor.

### 2. Open Collective (transparent finances)

For supporters who value financial transparency — every contribution
and every expense visible publicly:

**https://opencollective.com/aiwhisperers** *(to be set up)*

Open Collective is particularly suited for organizations that want
to document their contribution to public infrastructure.

### 3. Direct bank transfer (for larger contributions)

For supporters wishing to contribute through direct bank transfer
(typically larger amounts, organizational support, or specific
project sponsorship), please contact denis.czulinski@gmail.com to
arrange details.

### 4. Code, time, expertise

Not all support is financial. Substantive code contributions,
translation work, documentation improvements, security audits, and
constructive feedback are valuable forms of contribution. See
[CONTRIBUTING.md](./CONTRIBUTING.md) for details.

## What the funds support

Funds contributed through these channels support:

- **Maintainer time** spent on AiWhisperers development, documentation,
  and community support
- **Infrastructure costs** — hosting, domain registration, third-party
  API costs for development and testing
- **Tooling** — licenses for tools used in development (in cases where
  open-source alternatives are insufficient)
- **Conferences and standardization** — travel and participation fees
  for FOSDEM, IETF, W3C, and similar venues where the project's
  interests are represented
- **External audits** — security audits of cryptographic
  implementations, when budget allows
- **Future trademark registration** — EUIPO and national trademark
  filings for the AiWhisperers name and AiW* family mark

Funds are **not** used for:
- Salaries other than the maintainer's own time
- Marketing or advertising
- Equity or investment

## What supporters can and cannot expect

### What supporters CAN expect

- **Acknowledgment** — supporters above a threshold (see below) are
  acknowledged in the project repository (with permission, in a
  CONTRIBUTORS.md or SUPPORTERS.md file)
- **Continuity of effort** — funds enable continued maintenance and
  development of the project as an open-source commons
- **Transparency** — the maintainer commits to documenting how
  contributions are used (see "Reporting" below)
- **No influence trading** — funds support the project, not the
  donor's specific agenda. See "What support does NOT buy" below.

### What support does NOT buy

To keep the relationship clean and the project independent:

- **Support does not influence project direction.** Roadmap decisions
  remain with the maintainer, based on technical merit and community
  input.
- **Support does not constitute a service contract.** This is a
  donation, not a payment for deliverables. No SLA, no support
  tickets, no guaranteed response times. (For service contracts, see
  COMMERCIAL.md.)
- **Support does not grant special licensing.** All code remains
  AGPL-3.0. Commercial licensing (if needed) is a separate negotiation
  via COMMERCIAL.md.
- **Support does not grant trademark permissions.** Trademark usage
  is governed by TRADEMARK.md regardless of donation status.

This separation is important. The project is a commons; donations
sustain the commons; they do not buy parts of it.

## Acknowledgment thresholds

Supporters are acknowledged in `SUPPORTERS.md` (with explicit consent)
based on cumulative contribution:

| Tier | Threshold | Recognition |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Sustainer | 50 EUR cumulative | Listed by name in SUPPORTERS.md |
| Builder | 250 EUR cumulative | Listed with optional short note about why they support the project |
| Steward | 1000 EUR cumulative | Listed with logo (for organizations) or expanded description (for individuals) |

Anonymous donations are welcome and respected. No supporter is
listed without explicit consent.

## Reporting and transparency

The maintainer commits to publishing an **annual report** (each
January, covering the previous calendar year) that includes:

- Total funds received through all donation channels
- General categories of expenditure (development time, infrastructure,
  travel, etc.)
- Major project milestones during the year
- Plans for the year ahead

This report is published in the repository as `REPORT_YYYY.md`.

For Open Collective contributions, full per-transaction transparency
is available on the Open Collective platform itself.

## Legal and tax notes

This section is informational and does not constitute legal or tax
advice. Supporters in all jurisdictions are responsible for their
own tax compliance.

### For supporters

AiWhisperers is maintained by an individual (Denis Czuliński),
based in Poland. Contributions through GitHub Sponsors, Open
Collective, or direct transfer are **donations to a private
individual** — they are **not** donations to a registered charity
or public benefit organization at this time.

Implications:
- Donations are generally **not tax-deductible** for the supporter
  in most jurisdictions
- No tax-deduction receipt is issued
- For organizational supporters whose policies require tax-deductible
  giving, the commercial licensing path (COMMERCIAL.md) may be a
  better fit — commercial licenses are typically tax-deductible
  business expenses

### For the maintainer

The maintainer assumes personal tax responsibility for received
contributions per Polish tax law (Ustawa o podatku od spadków i
darowizn, art. 9, dla III grupy podatkowej / Personal Income Tax
Act where applicable). Supporters do not need to take any action
regarding the maintainer's tax obligations.

### Future structural changes

If at some point in the future the project transitions to a
foundation, association, or other organizational form that enables
tax-deductible contributions, this document will be updated
accordingly. No change is currently planned for 2026.
